Grantmakers are coming together to shift the culture of philanthropy from transactional to transformational.

By partnering with grantees to shape what we do and how we do it, we can best achieve our shared goals of social change.

We all know Grantmaker-grantee comunication is essential.

Research shows that grantmakers who are more connected to their grantees are more likely to provide the support that nonprofits need to be successful. And more effective solutions are shaped by building trust and tapping the knowledge and perspective of grantees and community members.

What the numbers say:

80% of funders say collaboration is important.

76% of funders evaluate their work.

A median of 25% of grant dollars now goes to general operating support.

53% of funders now ask grantees for feedback.

While GEO members understand an open approach, there are barriers that prevent us from building productive and authentic relationships with grantees.
